Time Flies Like an Arrow; Fruit Flies Like a Banana

October 18th, 2015

We are finished with week 2 AKA "Game Week". We focused on creating objects and methods in Ruby and making different games. We started out with a dice game called farkle. Then we created a version of the card game "War". Then for the weekend project was creating a version of the game "Blackjack". The games all use the command line to play.

Things are definitely ramping up quickly. Farkle was the first game. We had to create dice and and create the rules for a dice game that I had never heard of. We learned a lot of good tools for creating the games during the first class. Then we went to work. On top of using all the tools that we had just learned, it was also good experience of learning a project while I am working on it. War and Blackjack I am familiar with. Blackjack I might be too familiar with.

I spent a lot of time working out possible outcomes for all of the games. More time than I was originally anticipating. For War and Blackjack, I think it helped to be familiar with the games. I could think through most of the outcomes beforehand. However, it still seemed like every time that I would think I had it figured out I would play the game 5 or 6 times and a new scenerio I hadn't thought of would pop up. It was frustrating at times and there were a couple of late nights this past week. Iron Yard provides Tinker Coffee, and many of us in the cohort have taken full advantage of that.

Overall I am still enjoying the experience A LOT. That is good because like I said, there were some late nights. Each error message was initially frustrating, but figuring out a solution was like playing a logic game where I had to walk through all the arguments and try to figure out what the heck I did that caused the problem. It is still hard to get used to getting a lot of syntax errors when you start. When you fix one it just clears the way for the next error in line. You have to enjoy the small victories though, right?

I was really glad more people were able to make it to Iron Pints on Friday. Each Friday we try to have everyone get together without a computer in front of us so we can relax and get to know each other. Also, we had several people from last cohort able to make it. All three of them have job offers already! As it turns out, we have a wealth of knowledge in our class on sports, music, and tv shows. I have to figure out a TV show to get started on this week so I will probably pick up one of the many recommendations. So far since we have started I have been trying to pick up some new music, but I think this week I am going to reconnect with some of my favorites from high school and college now that I can actually listen to music while I work.

I am looking forward to next week. We have Git,HTML, and CSS coming up. Going to listen to some Ben Folds and Radiohead and I think my downtime TV show will be Peaky Blinders. I will keep you posted on how it goes. Have a great week!

October 11th, 2015

So this is happening. I am through week one of The Iron Yard Ruby on Rails boot camp in Indianapolis. I left my job in healthcare, where I have been since I graduated school, to learn a completely new trade. I have fortunately had a lot a lot of support from my friends and family on this decision. My parents and my girlfriend encouraged me to make the change if it was something I really wanted to do. I did put a lot of time and thought into the decision to come to The Iron Yard, but I still feel like I weighed all my options and made the decision to jump off a cliff!

So, what do I think of my decision so far? So far it has been a lot of fun. The Iron Yard director and instructor, Emily and Chris respectively, have been awesome in making this a smooth transition. The whole cohort met on Sunday to meet each other. We have a good team with a lot of experience in a lot of areas. PERFECT! Everyone seems willing to work hard, work together, and learn. Sunday night we also checked to make sure everyone's computer was set up correctly. Good thing too because Monday morning we jumped right in.

Don't get confused when I say it was smooth transition. It was smooth, but it was tough. We started out with a lot of the basics that we had to do for pre-work. I am not going to lie, I did the prework about 2 weeks before class started and didn't get a lot of chances to practice after I finished. This stuff is truly use it or lose it material and I had lost a bit of it. Each day we get three hours of lecture and then Chris sends out our homework.

That was Monday-Thursay. Generally with the help of classmates and Chris we figure out how to accomplish all of the homework requirements. It is exciting to get a problem, start with a blank file, and end up with a completed program that spits out an answer. Even though I know that my 5 hours of work is going to be done by Chris in 20 minutes tomorrow, I feel like I am able to do it and build on it moving forward.

Some people who are reading this are probably considering applying to or are about to begin The Iron Yard. Hopefully you will find this informative. I only have two bits of advice for you so far:

  1. Do the prework!
  2. Do the #{favorite_swear_word} prework!

Do with that list what you want. Remember I am one week in so what do I know? This is my first public blog so I am sure I will be updating and changing things as we move forward. I know the web page is kind of simple, but HTML and CSS are week 3 so things should improve. I hope you enjoy hte blog and good luck to everyone who is thinking about The Iron Yard!
